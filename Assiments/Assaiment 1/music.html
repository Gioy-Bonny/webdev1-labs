My Musical Journey

Music has always been more than just sound for me
it's a constant companion, a thread woven through the rhythm of my days.
My love for music started when I was just eight or nine.



I was immediately drawn to the raw energy of rock, metal, and punk,
and it wasn't long before I found myself behind a drum kit, fascinated by rhythm and beats.
Bands like Red Hot Chili Peppers, The Police, System of a Down, and Blink-182 became my first heroes,
their music shaping the soundtrack of my childhood.



As I grew, my ears began to wander into new territories.
By middle school, I discovered Italian rap and trap, and artists like Sfera Ebbasta, Tedua, Capo Plaza, and Mostro
quickly became part of my everyday life.
But my curiosity didn't stop there.
I began to explore beyond the popular hits, diving deep into
the history of different genres, eager to understand the stories and cultures behind the music.



Reggae became my next discovery.
I immersed myself in its rhythms and subgenres,
learning about the pioneers who defined it Peter Tosh, Gregory Isaacs, and, of course, Bob Marley.
Their lyrics told stories of struggle, hope, and life itself, and I found myself analyzing every word, every melody.



This journey eventually led me to modern reggae and to my fellow countryman Alborosie,
whose music immediately captivated me with its honesty, depth, and perspective on life.
My exploration continued into the world of Italian rap and hip-hop,
tracing its roots and evolution across the country.



I discovered artists like Neffa, Fabri Fibra, Club Dogo, Mondo Marcio, and Bassi Maestro
whose lyrics painted vivid portraits of lives, places, and eras
I hadn't experienced firsthand. Digging deeper,
I found the voices of Primo Brown, Noyz Narcos, Inoki, Nitro, and Mostro, each bringing a new layer of understanding and inspiration.



Among all of them, one artist stands out as having shaped me the most: Salmo.
His music — raw, honest, sometimes intense — has accompanied me through some of the most important moments of my life.
His lyrics have challenged me, guided me, and even helped me make some of the biggest decisions I've ever faced.



Music, for me, is never just background noise.
It's a teacher, a mirror, a companion.
Each rhythm I follow, each lyric I study, and each beat I feel is part of a lifelong journey
one that continues to shape who I am and how I see the world.
